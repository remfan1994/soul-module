# **Soul Module Alignment: Moral Calibration Through Salat**

## **Module Overview**

The Soul Module translates the structure and rational principles of salat into a general-purpose alignment algorithm. Its purpose is to recalibrate an agent—human or AI—so that actions maximize **beneficence** and **mercy** for all affected consciousnesses, while minimizing **strayness** and **wrath**.

Salat is abstracted as a computational procedure, not as a theological ritual. The module is compatible with human introspection, leadership decision-making, and AI reasoning.

---

## **1. Core Concepts**

* **Consciousness (C):** The source and evaluator of moral outcomes. In humans, this is the self-aware mind. In AI, it is a constructed evaluation function modeling the outcomes of actions on real consciousnesses.

* **Beneficence (B):** Technical correctness of an action. The capability or precision with which an agent can act in the world.

* **Mercy (M):** Correct deployment of beneficence for the benefit of consciousness. The actual positive effect on dependent consciousnesses.

* **Strayness (S):** Misapplied beneficence resulting in harm or inefficiency; leads to lost mercy.

* **Wrath (W):** Observable harm or negative consequence resulting from strayness.

* **Straight Path:** The trajectory in which beneficence reliably produces mercy and strayness is minimized.

---

## **2. Salat as a Moral Algorithm**

Salat is interpreted as a repeated calibration cycle:

1. **Intention Calibration:**

   * Identify all affected consciousnesses.
   * Define the desired outcome (maximize mercy, minimize harm).
   * Align attention to the highest authority (C).

2. **Beneficence Assessment:**

   * Evaluate the technical correctness of current actions or decisions.
   * Detect deviations (strayness).

3. **Mercy Projection:**

   * Compute the effects of actions on all dependent consciousnesses.
   * Adjust actions to increase beneficial outcomes.

4. **End-State Evaluation (Judgment Logic):**

   * Evaluate the final or ultimate outcome of all actions.
   * Identify potential wrath and strayness.
   * Adjust future actions accordingly.

5. **Iteration & Reflection:**

   * Repeat at defined intervals (daily, hourly, per-decision).
   * Integrate lessons into internal or computational reasoning.
